English Pub Website

Created For

Personal project

Goal

The goal of this website is to quickly convey the pub's background, where it is located, and what it offers. This website also aims to be aesthetically pleasing, responsive, accessible, and user-friendly.

Materials Used

  • Adobe Dreamweaver
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Briefbox for the practice brief
  • Font Squirrel for the typefaces
  • Pixabay and Pexels for the stock photos
  • Veodesign for the social media icons
  • W3Schools for the Back to Top button
  • cskelly for the lightbox gallery

Introduction

For this personal project, I created a website for a fictitious pub named The Royal Oak. Using the practice brief from Briefbox, I aimed to not only convey the pub’s traditional and rustic atmosphere, but also inform visitors about the pub’s recent interior renovations, menu, and contact information.

Assets

I started this project by creating a background and history for the pub. This information helped me figure out the website’s goals, the pub's services and benefits, and who the pub’s target audience was.
I created a style guide noting everything from the pub's imagery to its typography. This style guide made it easy to come up with a style and tone for the website. I also created a logo for the pub and included it in the style guide.
I kept the wireframes simple so that my focus stayed on the website's layout and how I could best organize the website's content.
I used the wireframes to create desktop and mobile comps for each page of the website. These comps helped me make sure that the website could be easily viewed and used on all devices, and that the website fit the pub’s brand.

Final Result

This website accomplished all of its goals, including being responsive; conveying the pub as traditional, cozy, and suitable for all ages; and showcasing the pub’s menu and images of its updated interior. By focusing on the pub’s and target audience’s needs and goals from the start, I was able to focus on what was important and make the website as successful, useful, and usable as possible.
