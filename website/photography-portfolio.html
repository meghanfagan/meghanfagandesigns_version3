Photography Portfolio

Created For

Mollie Duvall Photography

Goal

The goal of this project is to create a user-friendly and responsive photography portfolio that accomplishes all of Mollie Duvall Photography's needs and goals.

Materials Used

  • Adobe Dreamweaver
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Font Squirrel for the typefaces
  • Pixabay for the persona photo
    • All other photos were taken by Mollie Hemric
  • Veodesign for the social media icons

Introduction

I was determined to make Mollie Duvall Photography's portfolio website meet its needs and goals, and to make the website accessible and responsive. Accomplishing these goals ensured that Mollie's work would look accurate and visually appealing no matter how visitors viewed the website.

Assets

After asking Mollie some initial questions, I used her answers to create a logo, style guide, and business brief. The business brief was the foundation for the website and contained everything from typography to the color scheme.
I then created user personas based on many of Mollie's customers. These personas helped me think about the website from users' points of view and how the website could meet their needs.
I created wireframes in Adobe Illustrator for each page of the website. These wireframes helped me focus on how to lay out the website's information. I also created wireframes for different devices to ensure that the website looked great no matter what device it was viewed on.
After researching other photography portfolios, I created comps for each web page. These comps helped me determine the website's visual appearance and guarantee that the design fulfilled users' and Mollie's needs and goals.

Final Result

After Mollie approved the final comps, I coded the web pages. I made sure that the code was clean and organized, and tested each page. After a few weeks of hard work, the website was finished!

While I'm happy with how the website turned out, what really matters is that Mollie is satisfied with it. This is her website, after all, and my job isn't finished until the website accomplishes her goals.
